About Odissi Dance

Odissi is one of the oldest classical dance forms of India, originating from the eastern state of Odisha. Known for its graceful movements, intricate footwork, and expressive gestures (mudras), Odissi conveys deep emotions and tells stories through its performance.

Origins and Temple Traditions :

Ancient Roots:

Odissi is considered one of the oldest classical dance forms of India, originating in the eastern state of Odisha.

Temple Rituals:

Performed in temples as a form of worship known as Mahari Dance, which was closely linked to temple rituals and offerings.

Devotional Dance:

The dance form represents devotion to Lord Jagannath, the deity of the Jagannath Temple in Puri.

Spiritual Essence:

Odissi dancers were regarded as spiritual figures, and their performances were seen as offerings to the gods.

Dance Characteristics and Style :

Expressive Storytelling:

Odissi is renowned for its Abhinaya (expressions), which convey stories and emotions through dance.

Graceful Movements:

The dance incorporates a unique blend of fluid, curvilinear movements with firm and static postures.

Lasya and Tandava:

Odissi represents the balance between Lasya (feminine grace) and Tandava (masculine vigor).

Intricate Gestures:

Hand gestures (Mudras) and footwork are key to Odissi’s sophisticated dance vocabulary.
